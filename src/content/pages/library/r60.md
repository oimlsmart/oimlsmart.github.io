## Scope

This Recommendation specifies:

- The **metrological requirements** that load cells shall meet.
- The **technical requirements** governing their construction and performance.
- The **test procedures** used to verify conformance with those requirements.
- The **test report format** that documents the results.

It applies to **analog and digital load cells** used in measuring systems
subject to metrology control, including those integrated into nonautomatic
weighing instruments (OIML R 76).

## Accuracy classification

Load cells are classified by their maximum permissible error per
verification interval, into four accuracy classes:

| Class | Description | Typical application |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Highest precision | Precision laboratory balances |
| **B** | High precision | Jewellery, analytical |
| **C** | Medium precision | Retail, industrial weighing |
| **D** | Lower precision | Coarse industrial |

Each class is further specified by a maximum number of verification
intervals (`nLC`), from 50,000 (Class A) down to 100 (Class D).

## Contents by chapter

The 2021 edition is organized as:

1. **Scope and terminology** — defines the domain vocabulary and references.
2. **Metrological requirements** — accuracy class definitions, MPEs, repeatability, creep, hysteresis.
3. **Technical requirements** — construction, marking, protection from fraudulent use.
4. **Test conditions** — reference conditions, ambient requirements, equipment.
5. **Test procedures** — detailed step-by-step procedures for each test.
6. **Test report format** — the standardized form layout.
7. **Annexes** — classification tables, MPE tables, examples.

## SMART model coverage

The SMART platform models the entire Recommendation:

- All **42 requirements** with structured acceptance criteria (tiered, threshold, qualitative).
- All **18 conformance tests** with variables, procedure steps, and result forms.
- All **12 test report forms** with embedded calculations and MPE lookups.
- The full **terminology** (~80 terms) cross-referenced across the Recommendation.
- A **domain ontology** of ~30 OWL entities covering load-cell concepts.

The structured data is the canonical source for the interactive
application's R 60 functionality — requirements browsing, test execution,
form filling, and certificate generation.
