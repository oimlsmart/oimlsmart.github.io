## Publication

- **Code:** R 129
- **Edition:** 2000 (E)
- **Published by:** Organisation Internationale de Métrologie Légale (OIML)
- **Custodian:** OIML Technical Committee **TC 9** (Instruments for measuring mass and density)

## Scope

This Recommendation specifies metrological and technical requirements for
**automatic weighing instruments** that dynamically determine the mass of road
vehicles, axle-loads of road vehicles, or both, while the vehicles are in
motion.

It applies to WIM systems installed either:

- Permanently in the road surface (fixed WIM systems).
- As portable devices for enforcement (portable WIM systems).

## Accuracy classes

| Class | Tolerance | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| **0.2** | ±0.2% | High-precision static-equivalent measurement |
| **0.5** | ±0.5% | Enforcement (precision) |
| **1** | ±1.0% | Enforcement (standard) |
| **2** | ±2.0% | Statistics, infrastructure planning |
| **5** | ±5.0% | Coarse screening |
| **10** | ±10% | Indicative only |

## SMART model coverage

- **35 requirements** with structured acceptance criteria.
- **14 conformance tests** including the dynamic-vs-static comparison procedures.
- **9 test report forms** with statistical aggregation across runs.
- The full terminology (~50 terms).

A distinctive feature of the R 129 SMART model: dynamic tests produce
*distributions* of measurements across multiple runs and speeds, requiring
statistical aggregation computations (max, mean, std-dev) inside the forms.
